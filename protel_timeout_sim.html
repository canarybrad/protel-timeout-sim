What the model reflects

  • Lock scope: one distributed lock per reservation, keyed by hotel and confirmation number. Any concurrent action on the same reservation must wait its turn.
  • Wait budget: a waiter retries lock acquisition every 0.5 seconds, up to 180 attempts. After 90 seconds it gives up and the request fails with a "lock not acquired" error.
  • Plan length: realistic hospitality action mix — pre-check-in is 3 API steps, post-notes / update-guest / get-folios / post-payment-method are 2 steps, refresh is 1 step. Each step holds the lock for the full PMS response time.
  • Transient retries: ~10% of fetches hit a transient "not yet finalized" warning from the PMS and retry once after a 10s cool-off, inflating the time the lock stays held.
  • Hot-reservation skew: on arrival day, many flows (pre-check-in, payment method, post-notes, folios) target the same few confirmation numbers — the slider concentrates traffic to expose contention.
